# Theo van Gogh at Goupil & Cie

This note documents the intersection of the Van Gogh family story and the Goupil dealer records now in this platform.

## Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1873 | Theo joins Goupil & Cie, The Hague branch (age 16) |
| 1879 | Transferred to Brussels branch |
| 1881 | Transferred to Paris branch (Montmartre) |
| 1886 | Vincent moves to Paris; lives with Theo |
| 1887–91 | Theo sells works by Monet, Degas, Pissarro, Sisley through Goupil Paris |
| 1891 | Theo dies (January); Goupil records end |
| 1892 | Goupil sells Degas *Yellow Dancers* to Bertha Palmer — **Theo's successor handled this sale** |

## Why This Matters for the Platform

The [[Getty GPI — Knoedler and Goupil]] dataset covers Goupil 1846–1919. Theo's active period (1881–1891) sits in the middle of this. The Goupil records he managed include transactions in Monet, Degas, and Pissarro — all artists whose works appear in this platform.

The *Yellow Dancers* sale (1892) happened one year after Theo's death. His successor at Goupil Paris completed what may have been a transaction Theo initiated.

## The Human Story for the Demo

Your friend curates exhibitions. Theo van Gogh curated Impressionism for Paris — not in a museum but in a dealer's gallery in Montmartre. The paintings that are now in the AIC, the Met, and the Rijksmuseum passed through his hands.

The provenance record of *Yellow Dancers* is not just art history. It's Theo's professional legacy — what he sold, to whom, from which city.
