# Pricing Model Research Memo — Provenance Tracker

**Date:** 2026-06-19 · **Author:** provenance-strategy · **Length:** ~560 words

## Recommendation (lead)
Position Provenance Tracker as a **restitution / due-diligence research aid first**, with the
public globe as funnel. This is the only segment whose pain is acute, recurring, and budget-backed,
and it matches our actual asset: a reconciled, gap-flagged, sourced custody graph. Do **not** lead
with "insurance signal" — coverage is too thin to underwrite against (we already retired that claim).
Sell tiered SaaS: **Explorer (free)**, **Researcher €99/mo**, **Institution €999/mo**.

## 1. What museum registrars pay for provenance/collections tools
Incumbents (Gallery Systems/TMS, Artsystems, ArtBase) sell **collections-management** suites, not
provenance-gap research. None publishes flat pricing — all quote per concurrent user / institution
size, cloud or on-prem ([Gallery Systems FAQ](https://www.gallerysystems.com/faq_category/collections-management/),
[Capterra TMS](https://www.capterra.com/p/204323/TMS-Collections/)). Effective spend lands in the
low-thousands to tens-of-thousands €/yr per institution. **Gap they leave:** they store provenance a
registrar types in; none reconciles it across institutions or flags gaps. That gap is ours.

## 2. What a restitution law firm would pay for a gap-detection API
Provenance research for a Nazi-era (1933–45) claim is "protracted and requires substantial funding";
firms like Rowland & Petroff have recovered ~$70M in restituted works
([Center for Art Law](https://itsartlaw.org/resources/nazi_looted_art_cases/),
[Rowland Law](https://rowlandlaw.com/index.php/art-recovery/)). Research is billed by the hour and
often sponsored (e.g. Sotheby's funding the Louvre's program). A tool that pinpoints the 1933–45
custody gap saves billable hours on a contingency-driven case — these clients will pay
**€999/mo+ per seat** if it shortens research, and they buy repeatedly across cases.

## 3. What a fine-art insurer underwrites
Premium is driven by value, medium/fragility, storage security, **transit frequency**, geopolitical
location, and **claims history**; works that are "frequently moved, loaned, or displayed" carry higher
premiums ([Lockton 2026](https://global.lockton.com/gb/en/news-insights/key-fine-art-insurance-risks-in-2026),
[MyArtBroker](https://www.myartbroker.com/collecting/articles/insuring-art-practical-guide-for-insuring-your-collection)).
Provenance/authenticity feed valuation, and >60% of major insurers already adopted AI/blockchain
provenance tools by 2025. **But our coverage is too sparse to be a transit-risk signal** — keep this
as a future angle, not a v1 claim.

## 4. Realistic TAM
- ~104,000 museums worldwide; ~33,000 in the US ([UNESCO/Statista](https://www.statista.com/topics/7489/museums-worldwide/)).
  Addressable = institutions with real provenance burden, realistically the low thousands.
- Global art market ~$59.6B in 2025; auction sales ~$20.7B ([Art Basel/UBS 2025](https://www.artbasel.com/news/the-art-basel-and-ubs-global-art-market-report-2025?lang=en)).
- Digital art authentication/provenance software market: ~$320M (2024) ([Newstrail](https://www.newstrail.com/digital-art-authentication-market-is-booming-worldwide-major-giants-verisart-artory-opensea-masterworks/)).

**Bottom-up serviceable ARR (honest, not the headline TAM):**
~2,000 provenance-active institutions + ~500 dealers/auction specialists + ~200 restitution
practices ≈ **2,700 accounts**. At a blended ~€4,000/yr (mix of Researcher and Institution) →
**~€11M serviceable ARR ceiling.** Niche, but defensible and reachable.

## 5. Best positioning
**Restitution / due-diligence research aid** — the reconciled gap graph is the product; insurers and
museums are expansion lanes, not the wedge.

## Pricing tiers & rationale
| Tier | Price | Who | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----|-----------|
| **Explorer** | Free | Public, press, students | The funnel. Curated sourced stories build the credibility moat and feed corrections back into the graph at zero CAC. |
| **Researcher** | €99/mo | Independent provenance researchers, small dealers, journalists | Below the threshold needing procurement sign-off; one saved research day pays for months. Drives self-serve volume. |
| **Institution** | €999/mo | Museums, auction houses, restitution law firms | Multi-seat, API access, export, conflict-flagging. Matches incumbent collections-software budgets and contingency-funded legal work. |

**Moat reminder:** the value is the reconciled, gap-filled, user-corrected custody graph — never the
raw public APIs, which any competitor can also call.
